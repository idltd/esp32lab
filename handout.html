ESP32 Lab

A browser-based sensor platform for ESP32 — no app, no cloud, no accounts

ESP32 Lab turns a cheap ESP32 development board into a self-contained sensor workbench you control from any browser. Flash the firmware once, power the board, and connect sensors — the device hosts its own WiFi hotspot and serves a live web interface. Read temperatures, measure distances, detect motion, dim LEDs, and more without writing a line of code. It’s open-source, runs entirely on the device, and costs under £10 in hardware.

Sensors on display
  • DHT11 Temperature & humidity — single-wire protocol
  • DS18B20 Waterproof temperature probe — great for liquids
  • HC-SR04 Ultrasonic distance — measures 2 cm to 4 m
  • PIR Passive infrared motion detector — HIGH when triggered
  • KY-040 Rotary encoder — turn count & direction
  • Analog Potentiometer, light sensor, 0–3.3 V range
  • PWM LED dimming & buzzer tone via duty-cycle slider
  • Digital Button / switch input — LED / relay output

No app or cloud needed

Everything runs on the ESP32 itself. The web UI is served directly from the device over its own hotspot or your home network.

Browser-based OTA updates

Flash new firmware from the System tab — no USB cable required after the first flash. Works over WiFi from any browser.

Board auto-detection

Supports ESP32-C3 Mini and classic ESP32 DevKit. The firmware reads the chip model at boot and configures pins automatically.

Wiring quick reference
HC-SR04 — Ultrasonic Distance (5V version)
SensorESP32
VCC→5V (USB / VIN)
GND→GND
TRIG→GPIO 4 (direct)
ECHO→GPIO 3 via divider
5V ECHO pin will damage the ESP32 if connected directly. Use a voltage divider: 10kΩ from ECHO to GPIO 3, then 20kΩ from GPIO 3 to GND. TRIG is fine direct — it accepts 3.3V logic.
DHT11 — Temperature & Humidity
SensorESP32
VCC→3.3V
GND→GND
DATA→GPIO 4
✓ 3.3V safe — connect directly.
±2°C  |  ±5% RH  |  1 reading/sec max. Breakout modules include a built-in pull-up; bare sensors need 10kΩ DATA→3.3V.
PIR — Motion Sensor
SensorESP32
VCC→5V (most modules)
GND→GND
OUT→GPIO 4
OUT goes HIGH when motion is detected, LOW after the delay. Select Digital Input in the sensor dropdown. Sensitivity and delay are adjustable via the two trimmer pots on the module.
DS18B20 — Waterproof Temp Probe
SensorESP32
Red (VCC)→3.3V
Black (GND)→GND
Yellow (DATA)→GPIO 4
✓ 3.3V safe. Requires a 4.7kΩ pull-up resistor between DATA and 3.3V — without it the reading will fail. Uses the 1-Wire protocol; ±0.5°C accuracy.
